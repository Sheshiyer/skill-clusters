# Investor Psychology

Understanding what moves investors to help craft compelling narratives.

## The Investor Mindset

### Primary Motivations

1. **Return potential** — "Can this 10-100x my investment?"
2. **Risk mitigation** — "What could go wrong?"
3. **Pattern matching** — "Does this remind me of past winners?"
4. **FOMO** — "Will I regret missing this?"
5. **Story** — "Can I explain this to my partners/LPs?"

### Decision Framework

Investors evaluate along these axes:

| Factor | Question | What They Look For |
|--------|----------|-------------------|
| Team | "Can they execute?" | Domain expertise, grit, coachability |
| Market | "Is it big enough?" | TAM >$1B, growing, timing |
| Product | "Does it work?" | Traction, retention, NPS |
| Differentiation | "Why them?" | Moat, unfair advantage |
| Economics | "Can it scale profitably?" | Unit economics, margins |

## Emotional Arc Design

### The Hero's Journey for Investors

Map your pitch to the hero's journey archetype:

1. **Ordinary World** (Problem) — The status quo is broken
2. **Call to Adventure** (Vision) — There's a better way
3. **Meeting the Mentor** (Solution) — We have the answer
4. **Tests & Allies** (Traction) — It's working
5. **The Ordeal** (Competition) — We can win
6. **The Reward** (Market) — The prize is massive
7. **The Return** (Ask) — Join us

### Emotional Beats by Section

| Section | Target Emotion | Techniques |
|---------|---------------|------------|
| Hook | Curiosity | Bold claim, unexpected stat |
| Problem | Recognition, frustration | Relatable pain, quantified loss |
| Solution | Relief, "aha!" | Simple explanation, demo |
| Traction | Trust, validation | Numbers, logos, testimonials |
| Market | Excitement, greed | Big numbers, growth charts |
| Model | Confidence | Clear math, unit economics |
| Competition | Clarity, security | Positioning, moat |
| Team | Trust, connection | Credentials, story, authenticity |
| Roadmap | Anticipation | Clear milestones, ambition |
| Ask | Urgency, action | Clear terms, scarcity |

## Stage-Specific Messaging

### Pre-Seed / Seed

**Investor concerns:**
- Is the team exceptional?
- Is the insight unique?
- Is the market real?

**Emphasize:**
- Founder-market fit
- Unique insight/access
- Early signals (waitlist, pilots)
- Learning velocity

**De-emphasize:**
- Revenue (it's okay if small)
- Detailed financials

### Series A

**Investor concerns:**
- Is there product-market fit?
- Can it scale?
- Is the market big enough?

**Emphasize:**
- Retention/engagement metrics
- Unit economics trajectory
- Repeatable sales motion
- Market expansion plan

**De-emphasize:**
- Profitability (growth matters more)

### Series B+

**Investor concerns:**
- Can this be a category leader?
- What's the path to profitability?
- How defensible is the moat?

**Emphasize:**
- Market share trajectory
- Competitive moat deepening
- Path to profitability
- International/expansion opportunities

## Industry-Specific Framing

### B2B SaaS

**Magic metrics:**
- ARR / MRR
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR) >100%
- CAC Payback <18 months
- Logo velocity
- Expansion revenue %

**Narrative angles:**
- "We're the system of record for [function]"
- "Land and expand" motion
- Platform / ecosystem potential

### Consumer

**Magic metrics:**
- DAU/MAU ratio >25%
- D1/D7/D30 retention
- Viral coefficient
- Time spent / engagement

**Narrative angles:**
- Viral loops
- Network effects
- Cultural moment / zeitgeist
- Habit formation

### Marketplace

**Magic metrics:**
- GMV growth
- Take rate
- Liquidity metrics
- Repeat purchase rate

**Narrative angles:**
- "Winner takes most" dynamics
- Network effects (both sides)
- Disintermediation risk mitigation

### Fintech

**Magic metrics:**
- TPV (Total Payment Volume)
- Net margin per transaction
- Customer acquisition cost
- Regulatory status

**Narrative angles:**
- Regulatory moat
- Trust & security
- Embedded finance opportunity

### Hardware / DeepTech

**Magic metrics:**
- BOM costs / margins
- R&D efficiency
- Patent portfolio
- Production scalability

**Narrative angles:**
- Technical breakthrough
- IP moat
- Team credentials
- Strategic partnerships

## Objection Handling

### Common Objections by Stage

**Early Stage:**
| Objection | Reframe |
|-----------|---------|
| "Too early" | "Early enough for 100x, late enough to have signals" |
| "No revenue" | "Pre-revenue by choice, prioritizing PMF" |
| "Small team" | "Lean and capital-efficient" |

**Growth Stage:**
| Objection | Reframe |
|-----------|---------|
| "Competition" | "Competition validates market; here's our moat" |
| "Burn rate" | "Investing in growth with clear payback" |
| "Not profitable" | "Margin trajectory clear; profitability when we choose" |

### Universal Objection Framework

1. **Acknowledge** — Show you understand the concern
2. **Reframe** — Provide new context
3. **Evidence** — Back with data/proof
4. **Forward** — Point to future resolution

## Trust Signals

### What Builds Trust

| Signal | Weight | How to Show |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Traction metrics | High | Animated counters, charts |
| Customer logos | High | Logo marquee |
| Testimonials | Medium | Quote cards |
| Team credentials | Medium | Background mentions |
| Press mentions | Low | Logo strip |
| Investor backing | Medium | "Backed by" section |

### Credibility Hierarchy

1. **Revenue** — Hard to fake
2. **User metrics** — Engagement, retention
3. **Customer testimonials** — Named, with titles
4. **Partnerships** — Strategic relationships
5. **Press** — Third-party validation
6. **Team background** — Prior exits, domain expertise

## Cognitive Biases to Leverage

| Bias | Application |
|------|-------------|
| Anchoring | Lead with impressive numbers |
| Social proof | Show logos, testimonials, investors |
| Scarcity | Limited allocation, round timing |
| Authority | Team credentials, advisors |
| Consistency | Reference prior conversations |
| Loss aversion | Frame as "missing out" |
| Bandwagon | Mention other investor interest |

## The "Napkin Test"

Can someone explain your business in one sentence after the pitch?

**Test your pitch passes if:**
- [ ] Clear what you do
- [ ] Clear who it's for
- [ ] Clear why it's different
- [ ] Clear why now
- [ ] Memorable hook

**Template:** "We're {category} for {audience} that {key differentiator}."
