# Thoughtseed Business Loop

Use this reference when `autoresearch` is applied to business or product research instead of model training.

## Goal

Run fast, reversible experiments that improve Thoughtseed's commercial clarity without drifting into vague brand theater.

## Good experiment targets

- Homepage headline variants
- Primary CTA wording
- Offer packaging
- Proof and case-study framing
- Audience segmentation
- Discovery-call qualification criteria
- Workflow or delivery-system changes
- Content hooks and topic angles

## Avoid

- Changing multiple core variables at once
- Calling a result "better" without a metric
- Unbounded scope creep into full product redesign
- Mystical language unless the user explicitly wants it foregrounded

## Loop

1. State the research question in one sentence.
2. Define the baseline.
3. Pick one variable to change.
4. Define the success metric.
5. Run the test.
6. Record the result.
7. Keep, discard, or queue for retest.

## Suggested metrics

- Click-through rate on primary CTA
- Discovery-call conversion rate
- Reply rate to outreach
- Positive qualitative signal from target users
- Time saved in delivery workflow
- Clarity score from founder review

## Logging template

Use a flat table or TSV with these columns:

`id | question | baseline | change | metric | result | decision | notes`

Example rows:

`01 | Which homepage promise earns more discovery intent? | "Interdisciplinary innovation lab" headline | "AI systems studio for founders and operators" headline | CTA clicks | +18% | keep | clearer commercial framing`

`02 | Does a strategy-call CTA outperform a waitlist CTA? | waitlist CTA | strategy-call CTA | booked calls | -12% | discard | too much commitment for cold traffic`

## Thoughtseed defaults

- Business shape: hybrid studio
- Primary outcome: `thoughtseed.space` relaunch
- Commercial model: services funding products
- Brand posture: practical first, philosophy underneath

## Deliverable

At the end of a batch, produce:

1. A short summary of what won.
2. A short summary of what lost.
3. The specific changes to adopt next.
4. The next 3 highest-value experiments.
